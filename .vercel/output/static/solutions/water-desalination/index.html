Core technologies

Three solar desalination pathways

Empereal selects and designs the appropriate desalination technology based on the specific energy source, water quality requirements, and scale of the project.

  • Solar Thermal — LFR Desalination

    Linear Fresnel Reflector (LFR) solar thermal fields generate steam at 98°C to drive multi-effect distillation (MED) desalination. Patented direct saturated steam-generating technology with thermal storage for uninterrupted operation.

    Patented Technology

  • PV-Powered Reverse Osmosis

    Photovoltaic arrays power reverse osmosis (RO) membranes to produce fresh water from seawater or brackish water. Suitable for off-grid and grid-connected configurations, from 1,000 to 1,000,000+ litres per day.

    Grid & Off-Grid

  • Forward Osmosis & Hybrid Systems

    Forward osmosis systems for industrial effluent recovery and low-energy desalination. Solar-biomass hybrid configurations for round-the-clock fresh water production without battery storage in remote applications.

    Hybrid & Industrial

Patented LFR Technology

Direct steam generation for continuous desalination.

Empereal's patented Linear Fresnel Reflector technology generates direct saturated steam at 98°C from concentrated solar radiation — eliminating heat transfer fluids and reducing system complexity. The steam directly drives multi-effect distillation units, producing ultra-pure water at less than 5 ppm.

Thermal energy storage using phase-change materials ensures uninterrupted desalination even when solar irradiance is unavailable — enabling true 24/7 fresh water production from renewable energy.

  • Product water quality: < 5 ppm (ultra-pure)
  • Steam temperature: 98°C from solar field alone
  • 24/7 operation via phase-change thermal storage
  • Zero fossil fuel input — fully renewable operation
  • Scalable: 20,000 to 150,000+ litres per day per plant

  • Brackish water

Scope of services

What we deliver

Empereal's water and desalination scope covers the full project lifecycle — from initial source water analysis through design, EPC, commissioning, and long-term O&M.

  • Solar thermal LFR desalination

    Design, fabrication, and installation of Linear Fresnel Reflector solar thermal fields driving MED desalination units.

  • PV-powered reverse osmosis (RO)

    Solar photovoltaic arrays powering reverse osmosis membranes — grid-connected and off-grid configurations.

  • Forward osmosis water treatment

    Low-energy forward osmosis systems for industrial effluent recovery and water reuse applications.

  • Solar-biomass hybrid desalination

    Round-the-clock fresh water production combining solar thermal with biomass backup — no fossil fuel dependency.

  • Flue gas waste heat recovery

    Industrial desalination using waste heat from power plant flue gas — converting a by-product into ultra-pure process water.

  • Industrial effluent recycling

    Cooling tower blowdown treatment, boiler feedwater production, and industrial wastewater reuse systems.

  • Standalone & off-grid systems

    Decentralised community-scale water production for locations with no grid access or unreliable power.

  • Design, integration & commissioning

    Full EPC scope from source water analysis and detailed engineering through commissioning and authority approvals.

  • Long-term O&M

    Performance monitoring, preventive maintenance, membrane management, and water quality reporting for commissioned plants.

Applications

Six desalination application sectors

Empereal's solar desalination systems serve diverse end-users — from remote island communities to large industrial facilities requiring ultra-pure process water.

  • Coastal communities & islands

    Standalone seawater desalination powered entirely by solar — providing reliable fresh water without fossil fuel supply chains.

  • Power plants

    Flue gas waste heat recovery converting cooling tower blowdown into demineralized water — reducing industrial water consumption.

  • Industrial facilities

    Ultra-pure process water (< 5 ppm) for boilers, manufacturing processes, and industrial cooling — reducing reliance on municipal supply.

  • Rural & remote areas

    Decentralized community-scale water production without grid dependence — sustainable, affordable fresh water in underserved regions.

  • Resorts & hospitality

    Sustainable fresh water systems for net-zero tourism developments — integrated with solar PV and thermal energy to eliminate dependencies.

  • Municipal water supply

    Large-scale solar desalination for public water infrastructure — scalable from pilot plants to municipal-scale systems supplying thousands.

How we deliver

Our water project process

From source water analysis to long-term plant operations — every desalination project follows Empereal's rigorous, phased EPC delivery process.

  1. 1

    Source water analysis

    Feed water characterisation, salinity testing, site assessment, and resource modelling for solar field sizing.

  2. 2

    System design & engineering

    Technology selection, thermal or PV system design, desalination unit sizing, and storage integration.

  3. 3

    Fabrication & installation

    LFR mirror field or PV array installation, desalination unit assembly, piping, controls, and commissioning.

  4. 4

    Operations & monitoring

    Water quality monitoring, preventive maintenance, membrane management, and plant performance reporting.
